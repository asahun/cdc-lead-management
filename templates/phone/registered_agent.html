Call Script – Registered Agent (Dissolved / Inactive Entity)

Purpose: for dissolved or inactive entities where the Registered Agent may have a forwarding contact for the former owner or officer of [OwnerName].

1. Opening

You:

Hi, this is [ProfileFirstName] with LoadRouter here in Georgia. I’m hoping you can help with a quick clarification regarding an entity your firm was listed as the registered agent for.

2. Scenario Explanation

You:

We identified an unclaimed-property item reported under the dissolved or inactive business [OwnerName]. The state record shows it as a [PropertyType] from [HolderName], reported in [ReportYear] for roughly [PropertyAmount] (reference ID [PropertyID]). I’m trying to confirm whether you have a forwarding contact or representative on file for the former owner or officer.

3. Reassurance

You:

This isn’t marketing — it’s purely administrative. The State of Georgia is holding funds tied to that entity, and I’m trying to route the information to the proper former representative.

4. If They Ask for Your Business Registration

You:

Of course — our entity is LoadRouter, LLC, registered in Georgia. I can send the Secretary of State listing or link if you’d like to verify us.

5. If They Ask for Proof of the State Record

You:

I can email a screenshot of the record as it appears on Georgia’s public unclaimed-property site — it shows the entity name, holder, report year, and reference ID without any sensitive information.

6. If They Cannot Release Contact Information

You:

I understand. Would you be able to forward my information to the last known owner or officer on file? I can send a short note for you to forward — there’s no action required from your side beyond that.

Info to provide:

Name: [ProfileFullName]

Company: LoadRouter, LLC

Purpose: Unclaimed property record under [OwnerName]

Email: [ProfileEmail]

Phone: [ProfilePhone]

7. If They Still Refuse

You:

No problem — thank you for letting me know. Before I go, do you happen to know if the former owner typically had a forwarding address, email, or accountant who handled dissolution matters? Even general direction would be helpful.

8. Close

You:

Thank you for your time — I really appreciate the help. If anything changes, please feel free to reach out to me directly.
